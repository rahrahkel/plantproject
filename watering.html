Watering Basics

Watering is one of the most important parts of plant care ‚Äî and one of the easiest to get wrong. Many beginners assume plants need water on a strict schedule, but the truth is: it depends on the plant, the season, the pot size, and even the room‚Äôs temperature and humidity.

  • Check the soil first: Stick your finger about 1 inch into the soil. If it‚Äôs dry, it‚Äôs time to water. If it‚Äôs still moist, wait a few days.
  • Water deeply: When you do water, make sure it reaches the roots. Water until it begins to drain from the bottom of the pot.
  • Let it drain: Never let your plant sit in standing water. Empty saucers after watering to avoid root rot.
  • Use room-temperature water: Cold water can shock the roots. Fill a watering can and let it sit for a bit before using.
  • Adjust with the seasons: Plants grow more in spring and summer, so they drink more. In winter, they often need less water.

Light Basics

Light is food for plants ‚Äî but not all plants need the same amount. Understanding how light works indoors can help you choose the right spot for each plant.

  • Bright, indirect light: This is the sweet spot for most houseplants. A few feet from a sunny window (not in direct sun) is ideal.
  • Direct sunlight: Only sun-loving plants like succulents, cacti, and some herbs can handle full sun. Others may burn.
  • Low light: Rooms with small or north-facing windows still work for low-light plants like ZZ Plants, Snake Plants, or Pothos.
  • Rotate regularly: Plants naturally grow toward the light. Rotating your plant weekly helps it grow evenly.
  • Clean your windows and leaves: Dust blocks light! Make sure both your windows and the plant‚Äôs leaves are clean.
